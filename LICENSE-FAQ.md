# TrustDB License FAQ

TrustDB is licensed under **AGPL-3.0-only**. This FAQ is a practical project explanation, not legal advice and not a replacement for the license text.

## Can I evaluate or run TrustDB internally?

The AGPL permits use, modification, and redistribution subject to its terms. Running an unmodified copy internally is generally different from conveying a modified version or offering a modified program for users to interact with over a network. Review the license and obtain legal advice for your situation.

## What happens if I modify TrustDB and offer it over a network?

AGPL section 13 contains an additional source-availability requirement for users who interact remotely with a modified version. The complete corresponding source must be offered in the manner required by the license.

## Does calling TrustDB through HTTP or gRPC automatically make my business application AGPL?

The license does not provide a project-specific answer for every architecture. Whether separate programs form one combined work depends on technical and legal facts. Keeping an application as an independent program that communicates through a documented protocol may be relevant, but you should not rely on this FAQ as legal advice.

## Can I redistribute TrustDB or include it in an appliance?

Yes, if you comply with the AGPL, including preserving notices, providing the license, and providing the required corresponding source for covered versions.

## Is there a commercial license?

There is currently no published commercial-license offering. Organizations that cannot adopt AGPL-licensed infrastructure may open a [GitHub Discussion](https://github.com/wowtrust/trustdb/discussions) describing the requirement without disclosing confidential information.

## Does TrustDB make evidence legally valid?

No blanket legal claim is made. TrustDB can verify cryptographic relationships between content, keys, receipts, Merkle proofs, transparency-log state, and supported anchors. Identity binding, key custody, trusted time, process controls, jurisdiction, and evidentiary rules remain deployment-specific.
